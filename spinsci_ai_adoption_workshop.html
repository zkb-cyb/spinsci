Module 01 · 90 Minutes

Adoption Heat Map — Where Are We, Really?

Most health systems think they know their AI adoption state. They don't. This module replaces anecdote with evidence using a structured scoring exercise against every deployed SpinSci agent and workflow.

1A

Individual pre-work turned group calibration — surfaces the "I thought that was live" problem immediately.

1B

The Adoption Gap Visualization

Turn inventory scores into a visible heat map that tells a story — and creates urgency.

⏱ 30 min 👥 Full Group 📊 Heat Map Poster
Exercise Plot the Heat Map

Purpose: Make the gap between "what we paid for" and "what we're using" viscerally visible to every person in the room, including executives.

  1. On a large whiteboard or printed poster, draw a 2-axis grid: X-axis = Deployment Status (0–4), Y-axis = Business Priority (Low / Medium / High / Critical).
  2. Place a sticky note for each agent at its current status. Color-code: Red = 0-1, Yellow = 2, Green = 3-4.
  3. Draw a diagonal line from bottom-left to top-right — this is the "Expected State" line. Every sticky below it is an adoption gap.
  4. Ask: "What would it mean financially if these red agents were at Green?" Use the SpinSci ROI benchmarks (below) to quantify the gap on the spot.
  5. Photograph the board. This becomes the Adoption Heat Map artifact for the executive debrief.

Exercise The "Shadow Adoption" Check

Purpose: Uncover workarounds — places where staff are avoiding the AI and doing work manually that should be automated. These are symptoms of broken adoption, not broken technology.

  1. Each team member writes on a sticky: "One thing I still do manually that SpinSci is supposed to handle."
  2. Cluster the stickies by theme on the board. Common clusters: Post-call data entry, scheduling override, manual payment follow-up, transfer routing.
  3. For each cluster, ask: "Is this a trust issue, a training issue, or a configuration issue?" — label each cluster accordingly.

Module 02 · 60 Minutes

Barrier Archaeology — What's Actually Blocking Us?

Most adoption blockers are diagnosed as technical problems. Most are actually human, workflow, or governance problems with a technical symptom. This module digs to the real root cause using structured techniques.

2A

Barrier Taxonomy Sort

Classify every known blocker into one of five categories — most teams discover their technical barriers are actually human barriers in disguise.

⏱ 20 min 👥 Full Group 📋 Barrier Registry
Exercise Brain-Dump & Sort

Purpose: Surface every known barrier — no matter how small — and reveal which category is most loaded. That category becomes the workshop's change management focus.

  1. Each participant writes one blocker per sticky note for 4 minutes (silent). No editing, no judgment. Write as many as you can.
  2. Go around and read each sticky. Place it in one of the five category zones (below). Duplicates stack.
  3. Count stickies per category. The heaviest category is your primary adoption risk area.
  4. Transfer the top 5–7 stickies (most votes or most impactful) into the Barrier Registry that will feed Module 4.
🔧 Technical
  • EHR write-back not configured
  • SIP/audio integration gap
  • No real-time scheduling API
  • CCaaS routing not updated
👥 People / Culture
  • Staff fear job displacement
  • No one trained post go-live
  • Distrust of AI accuracy
  • No AI champion designated
🔄 Process / Workflow
  • AI bypassed in actual flow
  • Manual override is faster
  • Escalation path unclear
  • Scheduling rules not loaded
📊 Value / Measurement
  • No baseline metrics set
  • No dashboard for leaders
  • ROI never communicated
  • Success criteria undefined
🛡️ Governance / Strategy
  • No executive sponsor post-launch
  • IT owns AI, not business
  • Competing priorities (EHR upgrade)
  • No AI policy / acceptable use doc
2B

Five-Why Root Cause Drill

Take the top 2–3 barriers from 2A and excavate the real cause — not the presenting symptom.

⏱ 25 min 👥 Teams of 3 🔍 Root Cause Map
Exercise Run the Five Whys on Top Barriers

Purpose: The presenting barrier is almost never the real barrier. Each team drills one barrier to its root so the 90-Day Sprint targets the cause, not the symptom.

  1. Assign one top barrier to each team of 3. Teams have 12 minutes to run the drill independently on paper.
  2. Start with: "Why isn't [agent/workflow] being used as intended?" — then ask "Why?" to each answer, five times.
  3. The fifth "Why" is your root cause. Write it large on a sticky in a different color.
  4. Each team shares their root cause in 90 seconds. The room votes: "Is this the right root cause?" Revise if needed.
Five-Why Example — "Kaite is configured but A/R days haven't improved"
  1. Why isn't A/R improving? → Kaite outbound calls are running but patients aren't paying.
  2. Why aren't patients paying? → Kaite can't offer payment plans — only full balance requests.
  3. Why can't it offer payment plans? → Payment plan rules were not loaded into the Kaite configuration.
  4. Why weren't they loaded? → RCM team didn't provide the rules during implementation.
  5. Why didn't RCM provide rules? → Root Cause: RCM was not included in the implementation workstream — IT owned deployment, not RCM.
"An AI agent that isn't connected to your actual workflows can only have a conversation — it can't complete a task." — SpinSci Platform Principle
Exercise Impact vs. Effort Prioritization Matrix

Purpose: Not all barriers are equal. This matrix decides where to spend the 90-Day Sprint energy.

  1. Plot each root cause on the 2×2 matrix below based on: Impact on adoption (vertical) and Effort to fix (horizontal).
  2. Top-left quadrant (High Impact / Low Effort) = Do First. These are your 90-Day Sprint items.
  3. Top-right quadrant (High Impact / High Effort) = Plan for Q2.

IMPACT ON ADOPTION

✅ DO FIRST

High impact, low effort. Fix immediately — these unlock adoption fastest.

e.g., load payment plan rules, designate an agent owner, set up weekly KPI dashboard

📅 PLAN FOR Q2

High impact but significant effort. Schedule now, resource properly.

e.g., EHR write-back configuration, CCaaS re-routing, new agent training program

⚡ QUICK WIN

Easy wins even if lower impact. Build momentum and stakeholder confidence.

e.g., send staff FAQ, record a demo video, share one success metric with leadership

⏸ DEPRIORITIZE

Low impact, high effort. Park for now — don't let these drain sprint capacity.

→ EFFORT TO FIX (Low to High)
Module 03 · 50 Minutes

Champion Network — Who Will Drive It Forward?

Prosci research shows organizations that ran structured AI adoption workshops saw 450% better adoption rates than those that didn't. The single biggest predictor of success isn't technology — it's having the right humans as owners. This module builds that network.

3A

Power / Will Stakeholder Mapping

Identify who in the organization can move adoption and who is blocking it — including people not in this room.

⏱ 25 min 👥 Full Group 🗺️ Stakeholder Map
Exercise Map Every Stakeholder

Purpose: Every stuck adoption program has a small group of people who could unstick it — and a small group actively or passively preventing it. This exercise finds both.

  1. Each person writes the names of 3–5 stakeholders (internal) who affect SpinSci adoption on sticky notes. Include people not in this room.
  2. Place each person on the Power / Will Matrix (below). Power = ability to remove blockers or allocate resources. Will = current desire to see AI succeed.
  3. For every person in the High Power / Low Will quadrant: assign a team member to write an "influence strategy" — what does this person need to see or hear to shift?
  4. For every person in High Will / Low Power: these are your grassroots champions. Give them a formal role and visible recognition.

ORGANIZATIONAL POWER / INFLUENCE

🌟 SPONSOR & AMPLIFY

High power, high will. Make them your executive sponsor. Give them the Heat Map. Ask them to speak at the next all-hands.

🎯 ENGAGE & CONVERT

High power, low will. Critical. Find their objection: ROI, workload, patient safety. Address it directly and privately before the next leadership meeting.

🚀 ELEVATE AS CHAMPIONS

Low power, high will. Give them a title (AI Adoption Champion), a task (run peer training), and visibility. They amplify adoption at the team level.

👀 MONITOR

Low power, low will. Don't ignore — an ignored skeptic becomes an active saboteur. Keep them informed; don't invest in converting them right now.

→ WILL TO SUPPORT AI ADOPTION (Low to High)
💡
Key insight from competitive research: Nuance's DAX rollout at 600+ health systems succeeded largely because they embedded clinical champions (physician advocates) at every site. Hyro uses "AI Success Leads" — a named internal owner at every health system. SpinSci should establish the same pattern. The role takes 2–3 hours/week and is high-visibility internally.
3B

ADKAR Individual Adoption Assessment

Score the five conditions every individual needs before they will genuinely adopt a new AI tool. Reveals whether your problem is awareness, desire, knowledge, ability, or reinforcement.

⏱ 20 min 👥 Teams of 4 📊 ADKAR Profile
Exercise Score the ADKAR Adoption Conditions for Front-Line Staff

Purpose: Identify exactly where the individual adoption chain is broken for the users closest to the AI. You cannot fix what you haven't diagnosed.

  1. Think of a specific frontline role — e.g., call center agent, billing specialist, scheduling coordinator. Score each ADKAR condition 1–5 for that persona.
  2. The lowest-scoring condition is the bottleneck. Fixing conditions above a bottleneck has zero impact until the bottleneck is resolved.
  3. Assign an owner and a specific action for the bottleneck condition before leaving this module.

Module 04 · 50 Minutes

90-Day Sprint Plan — Commit Before You Leave

A workshop without a committed action plan is just a meeting. This module converts every finding from Modules 1–3 into a named owner, a deadline, and a success metric. The plan is presented to the executive sponsor in the room before closeout.

4A

Quick Win Selection

Identify three actions completable in the first two weeks that will create visible momentum.

⏱ 15 min 👥 Full Group 🏃 Quick Win List
Exercise Vote on the Three Quick Wins

Purpose: Momentum matters. Early visible wins convert skeptics into supporters and keep the executive sponsor engaged.

  1. From the Barrier Registry (Module 2) and ADKAR bottleneck (Module 3), list every action item that could be completed in 14 days or fewer with no new budget.
  2. Each participant gets 3 votes (dot stickers). Place dots on the actions you believe will have the most visible impact.
  3. Top 3 vote-getters are the Quick Wins. Assign an owner and deadline to each before moving on.
Quick Win Examples (SpinSci-specific)
  • Assign a named AI Adoption Champion for each service line using SpinSci agents
  • Load payment plan rules into Kaite configuration (CSM task — <1 day)
  • Create and share a one-page "What Kaite Does / Doesn't Do" FAQ with billing staff
  • Set up a weekly call volume / containment rate dashboard and share with leadership
  • Run a 15-minute live demo of SpinSci for the contact center team during a shift handover
  • Schedule monthly adoption review meetings with CSM on the calendar for the next quarter
  • Identify and fix the one workflow step where staff most often bypass the AI
4B

90-Day Sprint Commitment Board

Build the full sprint plan in three time-boxed waves. Every row has a named owner before the session ends.

⏱ 30 min 👥 Full Group 📅 Signed Sprint Plan
Exercise Build the Sprint Board in Three Waves

Purpose: Translate the Heat Map, Barrier Registry, and ADKAR findings into a concrete, time-bound plan that can be reviewed at every monthly check-in.

  1. Populate each sprint wave using the findings from Modules 1–3. Every action needs: Owner name, Success metric, Completion date.
  2. The executive sponsor in the room verbally commits to each wave. If they won't commit, the action is not resourced — adjust accordingly rather than write unowned tasks.
  3. SpinSci CSM commits to their parallel actions alongside the health system team.
  4. Photograph the board. SpinSci CSM digitizes and sends the sprint plan within 24 hours.
⚡ Wave 1 — Weeks 1–4: Foundation
  • Assign AI Adoption Champions per service line
  • Run ADKAR assessment for front-line roles
  • Fix top "Shadow Adoption" workflow bypass
  • Load missing configuration (payment plans, scheduling rules)
  • Set up KPI dashboard and baseline metrics
  • All-staff SpinSci briefing (15 min at next team meeting)
🔧 Wave 2 — Weeks 5–8: Enablement
  • Deliver role-specific micro-training (3 × 15 min sessions)
  • Executive sponsor presents AI progress at leadership meeting
  • Re-run Deployment Inventory — compare to Wave 1 baseline
  • Fix top-priority technical barrier (from Module 2 matrix)
  • Begin converting "Engage & Convert" stakeholders
  • First monthly adoption review with SpinSci CSM
📈 Wave 3 — Weeks 9–12: Acceleration
  • Re-score all 8 Readiness Dimensions — measure delta
  • Activate next SpinSci agent (highest-priority undeployed)
  • Publish ROI results to leadership — document wins
  • Recognize AI Adoption Champions publicly
  • Plan Q2 roadmap: next agent expansion and EHR depth
  • Quarterly Executive Business Review with SpinSci

Running this well

⚡
Energy Management

  • Modules 1B and 2B are high energy — use after breaks
